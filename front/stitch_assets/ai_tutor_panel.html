Hola! Ready to continue practicing the subjunctive mood? Last time we focused on expressions of doubt. A quick reminder: we use the subjunctive after phrases like "Dudo que..." (I doubt that).

Can you give me an example sentence? I always get confused when to use indicative vs subjunctive there.

smart_toy Linguist AI
Absolutely. Here is the breakdown: Indicative (Certainty/Belief): "Creo que es verdad." (I believe that it is true.) Here, you are stating a belief as a fact in your mind. Subjunctive (Doubt/Uncertainty): "Dudo que sea verdad." (I doubt that it is true.) Because there is doubt, the reality of the statement is questioned, triggering the subjunctive form of 'ser' (sea).
